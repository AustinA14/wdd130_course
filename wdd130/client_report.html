Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I thought about what type of website I wanted to make, and I decided to make an informative website that could help young adult men learn helpful relationship advice.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

I communicated through teams. Yes there were some issues, especially with getting the link to the website. I got it later than I would have liked. Also the lead developer didn't usually respond to messages from other people in the teams group.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

No, the title wasn't something I picked out, and the css is still not done for the website. It seems like the lead developer hasn't done anything since the week we worked on HTML.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

Make it clear that if they have any questions, I am totally willing to clear up their concerns. Also make sure that I am clear as to what the heading of the Home page should be.

Lead Developer Report

How did you manage your development team and comunicate the tasks for your junior devs?

Each week I made sure to finish my part early in the week and give my junior developers time to work on their pages using the structure I made for them. I told them when the header and footer formatting was done, and gave them the css structure as well. I asked them for updates periodically. One Junior developer never responded.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?

I messaged him on our teams group, specifically tagging his name if I had a question. He was always quick to communicate and therefore we had no problems.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?

I published the website to github the very first week and gave the link to the published website (not the repository) to the client.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

It was very easy. At first I wasn't very confident in my github skills, but now I know my way around the website and the desktop application fairly well.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

Working with my teammates. Since it is an online class, many teammates didn't respond until the weekend, which was very frustrating. If I was waiting on the lead developer to do their part, for example, I couldn't work until Saturday night at 8pm. If I tried to communicate, many teammates simply didn't respond.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

Creating the website where I was the lead developer. I wasn't waiting on other teammates to do their part, and I could coordinate directly with the client.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I would make it clear at the beginning to my team that we had to commmunicate well and get our work done at a decent time in the week in order to work efficiently.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

Slightly. I feel more confident making websites and using github. Some of my teammates were working with me professionally, while others seemed like they only did their work Saturday afternoons. I feel that this is contrary to how a real work environment would function.
